Blog 10: Bootcamp Learning Plan

My updated learning plan regarding Bootcamp.

25 April 2025

Bootcamp Update:

What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

My professional goal is to get a job as a web developer, where I could hopefully work remote and continue to travel. Alternatively I would like to get a job overseas to continue where I left off. My personal goal is to make games using the skills I have learnt in Dev Academy, but will most likely be in another language, using Unity or Unreal Engine.

What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

I think a strength of mine is that I can remain cool, calm and collected under stress. I think this will be helpful during group work and I can act as a rock for my teammates when the going gets tough. I know one of my limitations will be making code that is readable and easily understood by others as so far I have just been writing code that only I work on, and sometimes I don't understand it. Working on clear naming and structuring will be important moving forward.

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

Keeping to a good schedule will be a challenge I definitely need to stay on top of throughout bootcamp. I was really good the first 3 weeks, going to bed at a reasonable time and studying from 9-5 every day. I did let this slip at the end of week 4, staying up late working on my personal projects and getting carried away debugging. Although this was not too bad seeing as foundations is self directed, I really can't afford to do this during bootcamp as I will need to be up and working in the morning and being tired could let my teammates down.

What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I would like to improve my communications skills and overall confidence. I will need these skills for working in teams and avoiding any unnecessary conflict due to miscommunication. I will need to get better at receiving feedback, not that I react negatively, just that I avoid seeking feedback for fear of it being negative.

What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?

I expect them to be helpful and kind. I expect them to give feedback, negative or positive, but in a constructive manner.

What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp

I will avoid distractions and minimize unrelated projects unless I have ample time. My time is fully committed to this course and is my sole focus for the 9 weeks. I will get adequate rest and take necessary breaks so that I am always refreshed and in my best frame of mind.

Foundations Learning Plan:

What is your long term goal or career pathway?

I want to get a career in the tech industry that I can be proud of. One that is both challenging and has opportunity for growth. Being able to work remote would also be a bonus so I could work while traveling.

A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning

I believe some of my strengths are that I am a fast learner, I have a logical mind and I am cool headed under stress. These strengths will all be valuable in this course but may also be my limitation, as I usually like to figure everything out on my own instead of reaching out for help.

What skills (non-technical/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I would like to continue to develop my confidence in general, and in my abilities. My communication in person and online as I am usually quite reserved and don't really use social media. I would also like to improve my organisation and time management skills as this is an area I could definitely improve on.

A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme. This should include:

An explanation of how you will manage yourself to work productively and safely with other learners, Facilitators and industry/community representatives

I am pretty easy going and can get along with anyone so I have no worries about working with others. I am quite looking forward to it as everyone in the academy seems really friendly.

What you will do when things are building up and starting to get to you

I will take a step away from the computer, walk outside for a bit, or most likely hit the gym (if I need a long break). I have found in the first sprint this really helped to clear my head and I even figured out a way to solve the issue I was having. And if I find this doesn't help, I will reach out to my facilitator to let them know I am struggling.

A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way

I will first try to solve the issue myself, then try google, then reach out to the discord for assistance. I will use the time blocks and Toggl as a guide to how I am progressing. If I have gone over time I will reach out right away.
