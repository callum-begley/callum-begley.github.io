Blog 7: Cultural Blog - Introducing Te Whare Tapa Whā

The 4 dimensions of wellbeing

14 April 2025

Te Whare Tapa Whā

Te Whare Tapa Whā is a model of the 4 dimensions of wellbeing developed by Tā Mason Durie in 1984 to provide a Māori perspective on health.

The 4 dimensions are:

  • taha tinana (physical wellbeing)
  • taha hinengaro (mental wellbeing)
  • taha wairua (spiritual wellbeing)
  • taha whānau (family wellbeing)

With 4 walls, the wharenui is a symbol of these 4 dimensions. The wharenui has connections to the whenua (land, sea, natural world) which forms the foundations for the four dimensions

With Te Whare Tapa Whā in mind, I have created the following wellbeing plan:

Taha Tinana (physical wellbeing)

  • I will go to the gym at least 5 times per week
  • I will take rest days to ensure recovery
  • I will aim to get 8 hours of sleep every night
  • I will get 8,000 steps per day, 10,000+ when resting from strength training

Taha Hinengaro (mental wellbeing)

  • I will practice mindfulness through meditation every day
  • I will aim to get 8 hours of sleep every night
  • I will continue to count calories and have a balanced diet
  • I will reduce microplastics intake where possible

Taha Wairua (spiritual wellbeing)

  • I will climb a hill once a week and appreciate the view
  • I will look at the stars once a week and reflect
  • I will play music at least once a week and start writing my own
  • I will make an effort to listen to new music

Taha Whānau (family wellbeing)

  • I will make an effort to reach out to my friends more often
  • I will spend quality time with family

Whenua (land)

  • I will climb a hill once a week and appreciate the view
  • I will look at the stars once a week and reflect
  • I will make an effort to walk to places that are close
  • I will get my steps in by walking in nature, and appreciate the wildlife around me
