Blog 2: Identity, Values, and Strengths Exploration

An exploration of my Identity, Values, and Strengths

4 April 2025

Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

At my previous job I was kept on as a essential worker during the initial covid isolation. I had customers who were also essential that we needed to supply goods to keep them up and running. One customer asked me for a product and when I went to get it I was asked how imperative this was as it would involve bringing someone out of their isolation to supply. I had a big ethical dilemma as I wasn't comfortable bringing someone out of isolation, exposing them and their family to risk. I talked with my manager at the time to discuss this dilemma and we came to a solution that didn't involve bringing anyone out of isolation. Looking back now I think I definitely made the right call as the solution worked out for everyone.


Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

I went to Aquinas college which is a catholic college in Tauranga. Although I no longer practice religion, I believe they taught good values. The golden rule being; treat others as you wish to be treated. They also put a very high emphasis on education. While at Aquinas I made lifelong friendships with the people I went there with, we still hang out to this day. I also have a very loving a supportive family around me which I am very grateful for, especially now I am taking part in this course while living with my parents.


Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.

I believe some of my strengths are that I am a fast learner, I have a logical mind and I am cool headed under stress. These strengths will all be valuable in this course but I may need to be careful I don't get bogged down trying to figure everything out myself instead of reaching out for help.


Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?

I will need to stay motivated and accountable for my time studying as I can be easily distracted and go off on a tangent. I tend to get really into a topic and learn all I can quickly then drop it once it starts to get difficult. I also believe taking regular breaks is important as I can get caught up in a problem and spend too long on it which will eventually lead to burnout. During the first sprint I found that going to the gym helped me relax and even solve some problems I couldn't sort at the computer.


Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

At my previous job I was tasked with training some new employees. We had a few employees that had difficulties picking up the job we tried many different strategies to help, including checking over anything they needed, quizzing them on basics and regular meetings to see how they were feeling. I think the strategies used were good, however they didn't end up being effective as both employees ended up having to be let go. In future I would implement the same strategies and continue to show empathy. Looking back it was a difficult job and not everyone is cut out for it.
