Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset and how it influences learning

14 April 2025

Neuroplasticity

Neuroplasticity is the ability of the brain to form and reorganize neural connections, the idea that our brains are malleable and moldable, just like plastic. We used to think that our brains were fixed, especially in adulthood, but we now know that our brains are flexible.

How can understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefit you?

Just knowing your brain can adapt change and grow will help remove barriers to learning. Many people think I'm not smart enough, or my brain doesn't work a certain way. But if you know that the brain can adapt and change through training and reflecting, you won't put these barriers on yourself and your learning possibilities will be endless.

Believe you can and you're halfway there. - Theodore Roosevelt

How can I use the principles of neuroplasticity for my benefit?

I have already made a commitment to improve my physical fitness earlier this year and knowing how this can improve my learning capabilities further proves its importance. I will make sure when I am learning that I will minimize distractions to improve my focus. I will space out the learning over time and not rush ahead to avoid burning out. I will also commit to practicing every day to move new concepts from short term to long term memory.

What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity?

There are many things you can do to help implement neuroplasticity in your everyday life. Including:

  • Physical activity (walking, sports, gym)
  • Playing games (video games, board games, etc.)
  • Learning something new (languages, instruments, skills)
  • Practicing mindfulness through meditation
  • Making sure you are in a good learning environment
  • Getting enough sleep
  • Repetition and practice
  • Reflection

Growth Mindset

What is a growth mindset and why is it relevant?

A growth mindset is the belief that intelligence and skills are not inherited but come from learning, hard work and repitition. It promotes taking on challenges, repitition and practice and viewing failure as an opportunity to learn. This is very relevant to the Dev Academy course as no one is born knowing how to code, we all have to learn, embrace the challenge and not let failure bring us down if we want to be successful.

Something that surprised me

When first presented with this concept, I thought to myself that I do have a growth mindset. In the last sprint I embraced the challenges of the JavaScript Cafe stretches, and enjoyed figuring out the puzzles. However when I thought deeper I realised I don't think this way in all aspects of life. I have always thought I don't have a creative mind, and would always take the back seat in my band when it came to writing music. I just thought "My brain doesn't work that way so I won't try." Now that I have researched neuroplasticity and growth mindset, I want to start being more creative and maybe it won't be the best to start off, but with a bit of hard work and repitition I can increase my abilities and create something to be proud of.

How will I integrate growth mindset into my learning journey?

I will commit to putting in the work, practicing every day and reflecting on what I have learnt and where I found difficulty. I will not see failure as my downfall, but use it as an opportunity to learn and grow. I will also ask for help when I need it, and not see this as a negative, but as another opportunity for growth. I will implement this not only for this course but for all aspects in my life including in music and at the gym.
