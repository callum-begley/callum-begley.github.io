About me

Hi, my name is Callum.

I was born and raised in Tauranga. I am currently living in Papamoa beach, just down the road from the mount. I love living by the beach and these beautiful mountains. I try to climb one once a week.

My hobbies include travelling, playing and creating music, PC gaming and working out at the gym.

Travel

In 2024 I quit my job and sold all my belongings to travel the world. I started off arriving in Ho Chi Minh, Vietnam. This was a huge culture shock coming from a small NZ city, to a city with almost double the population of the entire country.

There are no road rules, or crossing signals, you just have to walk confidently out into the street and the sea of scooters will flow around you. One great thing about Vietnam is the food, I had a Banh Mi every morning from a street vendor. And nothing beats a cold beer on a 40+ degree day.

After spending a month in Vietnam, I traveled around Thailand for a month before heading to Europe to backpack around.

Europe

Pet Sitting

I was very lucky to discover an app called Trusted House Sitters. You stay at someones house for free while you look after their pets.

This allowed me to extend my travels and also visit Switzerland which I was going to miss as it is quite expensive.

I ended up looking after 13 dogs and 2 cats. Here are some photos of a few of my favourites.

(Left to right)
Malcolm and Bailie, two brothers I ended up sitting for a few times as they were just outside Edinburgh. Chump the Scottish Pug, Lexi the one eyed Frenchie in Switzerland and Molly the Labradoodle with a very long snoot.

Edinburgh

I finally ended up in Edinburgh, Scotland to live.

I was fortunate enough to get one really snowy day, my first ever apart from Mt Ruapehu. but unfortunately I missed the fringe festival, so I will need to head back sometime soon.

Unfortunately (or not,) I was unable to find a good job which lead me back home and to Dev Academy

Traveling is great and I would recommend it to everyone, but it does make you realize how good we have it here in New Zealand.

Lately, I like to record covers on my home setup but as I sold my guitars before traveling I only have one acoustic to play on. Previously I have been in a few bands, mostly playing rock and metal covers with a few originals.
